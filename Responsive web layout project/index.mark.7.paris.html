PARIS


Paris, the "City of Light," is a timeless symbol of romance and elegance. Home to iconic landmarks like the Eiffel Tower and Notre-Dame Cathedral, it captivates millions every year. The Seine River weaves through the city, reflecting its enchanting architecture and vibrant life. Paris is a haven for art lovers, with treasures like the Louvre and the Musée d'Orsay. Its streets echo with history, lined with quaint cafés, bustling markets, and haute couture boutiques. Whether savoring a croissant or marveling at its monuments, Paris offers an experience like no other.


England at the head of a 50-mile estuary leading to the North Sea, London boasts a rich history spanning nearly 2,000 years. Its ancient core, the City of London, originally established by the Romans as Londinium, retains its medieval boundaries and remains a pivotal financial center. Adjacent to it lies the City of Westminster, home to the national government and parliament for centuries. From its rapid expansion in the 19th century to its modern-day status, London has evolved from the world’s largest city during the Victorian era to the administrative region of Greater London, governed by 33 local authorities and the Greater London Authority since 1965.
